Ideal Project

Overview
I have chosen to develop a game within the game engine Unity. This game will be orientated towards the current trend of a sci-fi cyberpunk theme. The final goal of this game is to survive within a corrupt city. Taking the movie Blade Runner (1982) as inspiration, I will focus on the mellow vibe and shader/graphics technologies, as I have not yet experimented with these yet.
Motivation
Motivation for this project is trialling new technologies, and wanting to experiment with the forefront of computer generated realtime graphics. Unity contains many tools for these technologies. However, I also am inspired by the videogame sensation "Cyberpunk 2077" and Blade Runner (1982). The atmosphere which Blade Runner has inspired me, since Cyberpunk 2077 did not hold up to the original 1980/90s cyberpunk that I grew up with.
Description

In the slums of this megacity, billions of people live below the poverty line. They must break the rules to wake up tomorrow. On the streets, you can only be assured safety if you’re friends with the gangs. In this atmospheric world, you’ll be challenged every day with making non-ethical decisions to stay alive, slowly diving deeper into the underworld or trying to escape, to the higher heights. Betray your origins, or ruthlessly accept it?

Technologies being worked on will be procedural animation techniques. By applying “wanted positions” rather than absolute positions in a standard animation. Fewer things will be needed to animate and create a more fluid result. This will make animating climbing up walls and unnatural structures more enjoyable than what is seen in games like Assassin’s Creed, which animate between points. The method of this will include adding restrictions to movement, so while climbing through something the character will not be going through the ceiling.
Another factor will be adding “states,” where the character will move between. For example, stepping will move the legs between states, and the arms may remain in a single state and be affected by the hip movement.

Another major technology which will be worked on is rain and how it will interact with the environment of the world. The intention is to make portions of the world able to flood and will require the user to wait to continue. However, waiting will not be numbing where you will have nothing else to do, but you will be able to complete other objective which may achieve the original goal. The rain physics will be cloud based, which will be aimed to be as real feeling as possible. The cloud development will start as at a point and expand until it meets a dew point and will rain. This will also affect the fog which the camera will experience. With a poorly designed city comes bad drainage. This is something I witness in particular areas of Sydney during major storms, however the intention is to exaggerate it so much it gives an additional feeling of disgust for the landscape.

Through this idea, it will be required to create a system where the quests, goals, or objectives able to be achieved through multiple methods. Much like if you require a stapler, you don’t need to go to certain Woolworths store, but you can go to nearly any store. The primary objective is not to create a game which is easy to complete, or easy to speed-run, or, as a matter of fact, even enjoyable. The main objective is to experiment with technologies and ideas and create a realistic feel of the world – without compromising too much enjoyment for realism (ARMA series).

Tools and Technologies
The software required to complete this will be Microsoft Visual Studio, utilising C# programming and the Unity3D engine. Hardware will be limited to graphics components capable of DirectX 12.
Unity3D runs modern graphics techniques through DirectX 12 technologies. Therefore, the primary learning goal of shaders/graphics will require DX12. Unity also runs through C#, and all libraries/code bases are going to be self-sourced. This is excluding the default packages within Unity, such as ShaderLab. Outsourcing libraries is useful, however, only until the extent of wanting to complete work efficiently, and I as a learning journey I am looking forward to fault finding every individual bug and error that’s encountered.
Skills Required
There will be a large amount of software written to create this project entirely, and a lot of design aspects in the second half stage. The largest code will likely be the procedural animation since it will be code-driven animation. The more challenge is testing whether the codes feel natural and normal, so I will need to get someone external to play-test the project and feedback advice on possible improvements. The plan of this project is to be completely self-written excluding the engine itself, so it will take a long duration and possibly only one major technology will be completed by 3 months time.
Outcome
As previously mentioned, this project is primarily for learning and experimentation. With the factors of learning and experimentation, a major contribution this will make to my professional life is that it will be able to add to a portolio, as this webpage template may be one day. Learning graphic technologies is enjoyable, and I have recently been inspired by the artist Sakura Rabbit, and that they have recently been featured by the developers of Unity. My goal is to create new and interesting things, and become a master of my trade.
